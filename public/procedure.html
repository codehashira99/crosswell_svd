Experiment Procedure

  1. Set up the crosswell measurement geometry with source and receiver wells at carefully chosen depths to ensure thorough coverage of the subsurface area of interest.
  2. Acquire seismic travel time data across multiple source-receiver pairs by activating sources at various depths and recording arrivals at the receivers.
  3. Construct the system matrix (A) that relates travel times to model parameters, based on the geometry and an initial velocity model for the medium.
  4. Apply Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) to the system matrix to obtain matrices U, Σ, and VT. This step analyzes the stability and invertibility of the problem.
  5. Filter small singular values in Σ to stabilize the inversion and reduce the impact of noise.
  6. Use the filtered SVD to reconstruct the velocity/resolution model of the subsurface between the boreholes by back-solving the SVD system.
  7. Visualize the results with heatmaps and raypath diagrams to analyze model resolution, data coverage, and geophysical features.
  8. Iterate: Repeat the procedure with different acquisition geometries or SVD regularization thresholds to study their influence on model clarity.

Tip: Always check the resolution and coverage maps before interpreting velocity variations. Poor data coverage or inadequate filtering can mislead tomographic analysis!
